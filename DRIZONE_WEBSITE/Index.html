Potential issues arising when using a Korean-style shower:


Water damage: If water is allowed to splash onto the walls or other fixtures in the bathroom, it can potentially cause water damage over time. This can lead to mold growth, paint or wallpaper peeling, or other problems that can be costly to repair.
Proof: According to HomeAdvisor, the average cost of repairing water damage can range from $1,000 to $4,000.

Lack of proper ventilation: Inadequate bathroom ventilation can lead to excess moisture that damages building materials, causing structural problems, especially in poorly insulated or ventilated homes. It can also create a breeding ground for mold and mildew, which can cause respiratory issues for people with allergies or asthma.
Proof: Moisture damage can lead to mold growth within 24-48 hours, causing respiratory problems and other health issues, says the CDC.




Slippery floors: Another common problem with Korean-style showers is the slippery floors. Since the entire bathroom floor is essentially the shower area, it can be quite slippery and dangerous to walk on, especially for young children or elderly individuals.
Proof: The National Floor Safety Institute reports that slip and fall accidents account for over one million hospital emergency room visits per year.
