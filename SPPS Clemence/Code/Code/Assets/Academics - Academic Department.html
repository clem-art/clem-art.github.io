Academic Department

Mission

SPPS stands on a mission of independent learning. By providing a rigorous curriculum in a caring atmosphere touting small teacher-to-student ratio, students grow up to be independent thinkers. It is our foremost goal to prepare students in every aspect so that they can develop the confidence necessary for success in their next stage of learning.

SPPS teachers are experts with hearts. Teachers are passionate about getting to know each and every student. These strong bonds add to a vibrant community life and lead to exceptional learning in our classrooms and beyond.

All students, regardless of their interests or future pursuits, are learning to develop their written and oral communication abilities for future success as scholars. SPPS incorporates various teaching methods to further hone these communication skills so that students are able to think critically and express themselves logically as mature learners.

At SPPS, students complete frequent and varied writing assignments, and read literature of many genres. English classes are designed for close interaction between student and teacher; students engage in class discussion, learning to think independently and persuade others. Guided learning in reading, critical analysis, and effective writing help students develop skills that will serve them throughout life. Intense review of grammar and mechanics, and a rigorous study of vocabulary in the context of readings strengthen the foundations of English comprehension and may help students to prepare for standardized tests.

The Science Department at SPPS believes that a basic knowledge of science is an important part of being an informed citizen. In order to be prepared for challenges in college and professional careers, the Department encourages students to master a set of skills and practices in science. Students are challenged to solve scientific problems using diagrams, models and representations, pose scientific questions, demonstrate the use of math in a variety of scenarios, and connect a multitude of scientific concepts that demonstrate a greater understanding of scientific principles and theory. Whether students are studying biology, chemistry, or physics, the focus is on cultivating these essential skills and practices.

All courses in Social Studies Department strive to examine the subject matter, apply the knowledge and skills acquired to understanding and participating in today’s world. The Department offers a rigorous program with a range of courses available for every student through his/her career. The curriculum takes the students beyond memorization to a level of comprehension. Through classroom discussions and various projects, students practice skills to think critically and coherently. Students in all courses learn ways to communicate effectively through oral, written and presentations. Through the curriculum students develop an appreciation for the past, a respect for social systems, and a sense of civic responsibility. Requirement for the school diploma includes a year of World History or U.S. History.
